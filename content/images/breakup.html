The figure shows the pressure field of a taylor bubble about to break the interface up. The colors represent the scale of pressure along the bubble length, where the red color stands for higher pressure, while the blue one represents low pressure. {%- endmark %} The non-dimensional pressure scale can be seen in the figure bar. After the break-up, the bubble eventually would split in two bubbles and the their dynamics would be different of the single bubble, since the volume of each bubble are different if compared to the original one.
